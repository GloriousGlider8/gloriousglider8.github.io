My VSTs

Below is a list of all the VSTs I currently own, in the order I bought them.
I have also rated each one on a one-to-five scale and recorded the disk
space they take.

50 GB

Kontakt 8

I bought Kontakt 8 second-hand, along with Komplete 15
Select: Electronic for £86. Quite a good deal! Kontakt is really
powerful, and has really good libraries! The problem is the
factory content, with its heavy reverb and little velocity ranges.

Don't get me wrong, I do love Kontakt, and use it in most
of my songs, it's just the fact that you have to pay extra to get
good sounds.

1 GB

Massive X

As I said before, I got Massive X as part of a Komplete Select
bundle. Which does help its rankings a little. Anyways, Massive X
feels more like an experiment than an actual product, it has a
very small factory library (although it is very customisable), and
is hyper-specific with the genres it targets.

I wouldn't get this if it weren't part of a bundle. It's limited in
the defaults it has to show you, and, as a preset surfer, factory
content is really important to me.

135 GB

SampleTank 4

SampleTank 4 was the third VST I bought. I bought it while it
was on discount (€44.99), so it costed even less than Kontakt!
You get much more instruments and they are generally higher
quality than Kontakt.

I am not kidding, this took me 2 days to download!
Overall, an amazing VST.

35 GB

HALion 7

HALion 7, priced at £290, is a bit more on the expensive side,
but is incredibly powerful and sounds great by default. You
get an expansive sound library, covering all genres, and a load
of customisation.

Is it expensive? Yes. It is worth it? Also yes.
